Restoration

The 1966 Chevrolet CruisAire had been sitting in a field in Rye, Colorado since the 1980s. The last trip it took was to the 1974 Spokane World's Fair as well as Banff, Canada. Calvin Hunter's parents before that had taken the CruisAire to Alaska in the late 60s. The family originally wanted the CruisAire to be thrown out; however, Calvin Hunter held on to it on his ranch, Rock Meadow Ranch, in Rye, Colorado.

Leigh Hunter, Calvin’s daughter-in-law, wondering if it had value, posted on a Chevy van site on Facebook inquiring about the van. The post got numerous responses, informing the family of its rarity and value. Leigh then looked up vintage restoration companies and found one very close by in Cañon City, Colorado. The company, Retro Restorations, run by Terry and Larry, only takes on unique projects. The family emailed Terry and Larry, and the brothers went to look at the CruisAire. They agreed to take it on and hauled it to their shop on a snowy day in December 2020.

The CruisAire was buried to the axles in dirt because of the time it had been sitting in a field. The tailpipe had a nest of mice within it from the neglect it endured. In the field, it was like a time capsule, tea cups hanging on their pegs, WWII army silverware, vintage aluminum foil, washboards, and maps decorated the van. The van has two bunks and a place to hang a hammock. One of the coolest parts that was still intact was the steering wheel, which is a double-ring vintage steering wheel that would be impossible to replace. Once in the shop, they realized how extensive the damage really was. Larry and Terry informed the family that one more winter season in the harsh elements of Colorado and the CruisAire would've been irreparable.

They began with structural elements. This included the floor, the fiberglass, and vintage white-wall tires. The biggest problem was that the floor had been rusted through. Larry and Terry used a ton of ingenuity to repair it. To replace the windows, they found them from a boat company because the specific type of windows aren't stocked by RV companies. They found the original vintage fabric that had been on the upholstery to replace the old rotted fabric. The restoration process began in the Spring of 2021 and finished in January of 2024.
